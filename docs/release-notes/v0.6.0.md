# Haspel v0.6.0 — 2026-05-24

## Summary

The shopping side of Haspel stops shipping a frozen parts list and starts pulling
the real thing from the stores of the printers you own — **on demand, when you
ask for it**. v0.6.0 adds the brand-store fetch, a multi-store shopping view, and
a staleness prompt so a months-old list reminds you to refresh.

## Highlights

- **On-demand brand-store shopping.** The preloaded Bambu parts/consumables seeds
  are gone (they ship empty now). Instead, the **Shopping** page fetches a brand's
  store contents into a per-install `store-lists.json` when you click **Update
  store** — never automatically, so it costs no tokens or bandwidth until you ask.
- **Real prices, no AI.** For Bambu (a Next.js storefront) the helper reads the
  P2S / AMS 2 Pro collection pages for product handles, then each product page's
  embedded schema.org data for the **name, EUR price, and SKU** — categorised by
  keyword. ~70 items in about 2 seconds, no model call. Prices show where the
  store lists them; out-of-stock parts simply have none.
- **Fetched from your machine.** The fetch runs from the helper on your own PC, so
  it reaches the EU store normally (cloud/datacenter IPs get blocked). Other
  brands are best-effort from a store URL you provide, with a clean "add items
  manually" fallback when a store can't be read.
- **Multi-store + filter.** A brand-store dropdown, an editable store URL
  (prefilled with the brand's EU store base), a per-store filter, and a "last
  updated N days ago" indicator that turns amber when stale.
- **30-day staleness prompt.** A store list older than 30 days prompts: **Update
  now? / No / Ask me in a week / Disable this notification.**
- **Notification management.** Disabling or snoozing a prompt is tracked in a
  general keyed notification state; **Settings → Notifications** lists what's
  disabled/snoozed and lets you re-enable individually or reset all.

## Changed

- `data/catalog/{replacement-parts,consumables}.json` now ship empty. The catalog
  of P2S replacement parts is sourced live from the store instead. The 65-model
  **printer spec** seed (`printers.json`) is unaffected.

## Fixed

- **Printer-catalog brand-store links pointed at the US store.** All 10 Bambu
  `store_url`s switched from `us.store.bambulab.com` to `eu.store.bambulab.com`
  (and the X1-Carbon slug corrected), verified to resolve.

## Known issues / limitations

- **Prices are only as complete as the store shows.** Out-of-stock Bambu parts
  render no price, so those items list a name + link but no EUR figure. A reseller
  price overlay (e.g. 3DJake) is possible future work.
- **Non-Bambu brands are best-effort.** Only Bambu has a verified end-to-end
  fetch; other brands depend on a usable store URL and may fall back to manual
  entry (Voron has no store at all).
- The store can rate-limit rapid repeated fetches; an occasional intentional
  update is fine.

## Deployment notes

- No data migration. The first time you open Shopping, lists are empty until you
  click **Update store**. Existing `data/catalog` parts/consumables seeds are now
  empty by design.
